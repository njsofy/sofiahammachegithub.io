Video Game Addiction Signs and Effects
Site Author
 Dr.Shriver


Abstract
 The sitediscusses video game addiction and its recognition as a mental health disorder by the World Health Organization. It emphasizes that the majority of individuals who enjoy video games do not suffer from addiction, and the disorder is characterized by compulsive use despite negative consequences. Signs of video game addiction include changes in behavior, mood, social isolation, and withdrawal when not playing. Physical consequences may include pain, sleep disorders, and long-term health issues. Treatment options include psychological techniques like cognitive-behavioral therapy, and there's ongoing research on medications.

 The Health Effects of too much Gaming
Site Author
 Peter Grinspoon
Abstract
 The site discusses the potential harms associated with excessive video gaming and emphasizes the importance of moderation. While acknowledging the benefits of gaming, such as social interaction and cognitive benefits, it highlights various health risks, including repetitive stress injuries like carpal tunnel syndrome, "Gamer's thumb," and obesity. The article also addresses psychological issues like gaming addiction, sleep deprivation, depression, aggression, and anxiety linked to excessive gaming. It suggests that moderation, education on injury prevention, and engaging in healthy activities can mitigate these risks and promote a balanced approach to gaming.
